About RationShop-Tracker

Public Distribution System

The goal of the Public Distribution System in Tamil Nadu is to ensure food security to all citizens, particularly poor people, by making available essential commodities of good quality at affordable prices every month, through fair price shops which are easily accessible.

Since 1972, Ration shops, also known as Fair Price Shops (FPSs), are government-run retail outlets in India that provide essential commodities like food grains (rice, wheat), sugar, and kerosene at subsidized prices to the poor and economically weaker sections of society. Operated under the Public Distribution System (PDS), these shops require a Ration Card for eligible families to purchase these subsidized goods, which are sold at a price lower than the market rate called the "issue price".
